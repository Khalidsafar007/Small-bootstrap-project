MARVEL CINEMATIC UNIVERSE

What is MCU?

The Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU) is an American media franchise and shared universe centered on a series of superhero films produced by Marvel Studios. The films are based on characters that appear in American comic books published by Marvel Comics. The franchise also includes television series, short films, digital series, and literature. The shared universe, much like the original Marvel Universe in comic books, was established by crossing over common plot elements, settings, cast, and characters.

DC Comics

What is DC?

DC, short for Detective Comics, is a renowned American comic book publisher and a key player in the global comic book industry. Established in 1934, it has given life to iconic superheroes like Superman, Batman, Wonder Woman, The Flash, and Aquaman, each with their unique abilities and compelling storylines. DC's characters coexist in a shared universe known as the DC Universe, forming teams like the Justice League. The company has expanded beyond comics into various media, including television, film, and video games, with the DC Extended Universe featuring prominently on the big screen. DC's cultural impact extends far beyond its origins, influencing generations with its diverse array of characters and engaging narratives across multiple genres.
